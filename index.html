I'm Daniel Kalla and I made this data visualization because I'm passionate about global health.

In the United States, I feel most people don't think much about diseases like tuberculosis and malaria, even though they kill large numbers of people every year worldwide and individual people can make a significant difference is fighting them. Malaria in particular is a very solvable problem, that just lacks money to address.
For that reason, I donate 10% of my income to global health causes like malaria.

These maps represent the number of deaths or infections from diseases below. All maps use the same scale, and one shade darker represents a roughly threefold increase in disease. (Dark gray represents no data.) Enjoy!
